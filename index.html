Category I

The Masters of Nonsense & Whimsy

These poets defined the genre with wordplay, invented creatures, and surreal logic.

🐇
1832–1898

Lewis Carroll

Master of Nonsense Verse

Best known for Alice's Adventures in Wonderland, he penned iconic nonsense poems like "Jabberwocky" and "The Walrus and the Carpenter."

Nonsense Wordplay Victorian
🦉
1812–1888

Edward Lear

Father of Limericks

Often called the "Father of Limericks," his most famous work is "The Owl and the Pussy-cat," a beloved adventure in verse.

Limericks Whimsy Illustration
🎩
1904–1991

Dr. Seuss

Theodor Seuss Geisel

Revolutionized children's literacy with trisyllabic meter and imaginative characters in classics like The Cat in the Hat.

Rhyme Illustration Literacy
Category II

The "Golden Age" & Narrative Poets

Capturing the essence of childhood through evocative imagery and storytelling.

🏴‍☠️
1850–1894

Robert Louis Stevenson

Poet of Childhood Wonder

His collection A Child's Garden of Verses includes staples like "My Shadow" and "The Land of Counterpane."

Adventure Imagination Victorian
🧸
1882–1956

A. A. Milne

Creator of Winnie-the-Pooh

Beyond Pooh, he wrote beloved poetry collections for children, such as When We Were Very Young.

Gentle Verse Childhood Warmth
🌹
1830–1894

Christina Rossetti

Victorian Lyricist

While a serious Victorian poet, her collection Sing-Song: A Nursery Rhyme Book contains delicate, rhythmic verses for children.

Lyrical Nursery Rhyme Victorian
"The time has come," the Walrus said,
"To talk of many things…"

Category III

Contemporary & Humorous Poets

Making poetry accessible and hilarious for today's young readers.

🌳
1930–1999

Shel Silverstein

Master of Subversive Humor

A household name known for his subversive humor in Where the Sidewalk Ends and A Light in the Attic.

Humor Illustration Beloved
🎪
1940–

Jack Prelutsky

First U.S. Children's Poet Laureate

Famous for high-energy, silly works like The New Kid on the Block, he makes poetry thrilling for kids.

Energy Silly Laureate
🍫
1916–1990

Roald Dahl

Dark Wit & Twisted Tales

Known for his dark wit, his Revolting Rhymes provides twisted comic retellings of traditional fairy tales.

Dark Humor Fairy Tales Subversive
🐻
1946–

Michael Rosen

British Children's Laureate

Widely loved for his performance-oriented poetry and the classic We're Going on a Bear Hunt.

Performance British Classic

🌍
Contemporary

Naomi Shihab Nye

Young People's Poet Laureate

A former Young People's Poet Laureate whose work often focuses on empathy and everyday wonders.

Empathy Multicultural Laureate

Nikki Giovanni

Voice of Family & Heritage

An influential poet who has written several acclaimed collections for children, focusing on themes of family and heritage.

Heritage Family Influential

Charles R. Smith Jr.

2025 NCTE Award Recipient

Recipient of the 2025 NCTE Award for Excellence in Poetry for Children, recognized for dynamic combinations of poetry and photography.

Photography Dynamic Award-Winner

Русская поэзия

Russian Children's Poets

The giants of Russian-language poetry for children — beloved across generations.

🐊
1882–1969

Korney Chukovsky

Корней Чуковский

The founding father of Russian children's literature. His rhythmic, fantastical poems like Moydodyr and The Crocodile remain cherished classics.

Classic Fantastical Pioneer
🧸
1906–1981

Agnia Barto

Агния Барто

Beloved for her gentle, memorable quatrains about childhood. Every Russian-speaking child knows her "Toys" cycle — Mishka, Bunny, and Ball.

Gentle Childhood Iconic
📬
1887–1964

Samuil Marshak

Самуил Маршак

A master translator and original poet. His playful verses, riddles, and translations of English nursery rhymes bridged cultures for young readers.

Translator Riddles Playful
🎭
1913–2009

Sergei Mikhalkov

Сергей Михалков

Author of the beloved Uncle Styopa series and lyricist of the Russian national anthem. His children's verse is witty, warm, and wonderfully theatrical.

Theatrical Uncle Styopa Witty
💡
1918–2000

Boris Zakhoder

Борис Заходер

The beloved translator who brought Winnie-the-Pooh to Russian children, and an original poet known for whimsical animal verses.

Winnie-the-Pooh Animals Translator
🌙
1930–

Genrikh Sapgir

Генрих Сапгир

An avant-garde poet who brought surreal, experimental playfulness to Russian children's verse with inventive wordplay and absurdist humor.

Avant-Garde Surreal Wordplay
